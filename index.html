Conway's Game of Life

Scientific Name:

Cells in Conway's Game of Life

Discovered by / at:

John Horton Conway, in 1970

Family:

Cellular Automaton (perhaps).

Habitat:

Mostly in two-dimensional rectangular gridded coordinate systems.

Appearance:

Simple square with two different colors. Typically, black indicates dead and white indicates white.

Key Characteristics:

  • Macroscopic visualization of communities is randomized.
  • It changes based on the states of neighboring cells.
  • A group of cells can form stable, oscillating, or moving patterns.

What we know so far about Cells in Conway's Game of Life? Though the rules of the iteration are simple: a live cell survives with 2-3 neighbors, while a dead cell becomes alive with exactly 3 neighbors. Cells die from loneliness (fewer than 2 neighbors) or overpopulation (more than 3 neighbors). Those adorabal cells can generate complex patterns.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature In general, initially these cells are not interactive. However, I have made these cells totally interactive! Mode 1 will automatically generate a randomized initial situation where observers can add live cells to influence evolution by controlling a mysterious device with the mouse. Mode 2 allows observers to set an initial “seed” and observe its evolution. If you are interested in it and want to explore more, you can refer to https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conway%27s_Game_of_Life#Examples_of_patterns for more interesting patterns.
